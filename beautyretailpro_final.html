How to Sell a Perfume: 5 Things Every Great Advisor Does

The best fragrance advisors don't push products — they listen, understand, and guide. Here's what separates great from average.

James Halford — March 2024 — 4 min read — Level B1

Selling a perfume is not about pushing a product. It's about listening, understanding, and guiding. The best fragrance advisors in the world — from Sephora to Harrods — follow the same five principles. Here's what makes them exceptional.

01

They ask before they speak.

A great advisor never starts with a product. They start with a question: "Who is this for?" or "What do you usually wear?" The customer's answer gives them everything they need. Listening is the most powerful sales tool there is.

"You have two ears and one mouth. Use them in that order."
02

They reformulate what they hear.

When a customer says "I don't know what she likes", a skilled advisor responds: "So what you're telling me is — you need something versatile and safe." Reformulating shows the customer they have been understood. It also builds trust instantly.

03

They make the customer smell, not think.

Perfume is sensory. Great advisors use descriptive, vivid language: "This one opens fresh, like cold air on a mountain morning — then it dries down to something warm and woody." They paint a picture. Abstract technical terms like 'top note' and 'base note' mean nothing to most customers.

"Don't describe a perfume. Describe the feeling of wearing it."
04

They handle objections without pressure.

"It's a little expensive" is not a problem — it's an opportunity. A confident advisor says: "Absolutely. We also have a travel size — same fragrance, perfect for a first gift." They offer a solution, not an apology. They never push.

05

They build a relationship, not just a sale.

The best advisors remember that a customer who loves their experience will return. They make the person feel welcomed, respected, and understood — not just sold to. A satisfied customer in a fragrance boutique spends on average 40% more on their second visit than on their first.

The bottom line

Great perfume advisors don't sell fragrance. They sell confidence, emotion, and the right gift for the right person — at exactly the right moment.
